MOTORSPORT APPAREL DIVISION

Precision Motorsport Wear Systems

Designed for high-performance racing environments, our motorsport apparel combines advanced fabric engineering with precision construction to deliver safety, durability, and unrestricted movement. From professional racing suits to teamwear, we develop garments that meet demanding performance standards while ensuring comfort under extreme conditions.

Motorbike Suits

Full-body protective garments for motorcycle riders, available as one-piece racing suits or two-piece jacket and pants sets. Engineered to prevent abrasion injuries and absorb impact across all riding styles.

  • • Materials – Full-grain cowhide or kangaroo leather, Cordura/Kevlar textile blends, stretch panels at shoulders, knees, and back
  • • Key features – CE-approved armor, pre-curved limbs, knee pucks, perforated panels, YKK zippers, double/triple stitching
  • • Sub-categories – Race suits, touring suits, adventure suits, and vintage/retro suits
  • • Typical users – Sportbike riders, track day participants, long-distance tourers, adventure and motocross riders

Leather Jackets

Versatile outerwear made from animal hides or synthetic leather, balancing style with durability. Used for fashion, motorcycling, industrial work, and uniform applications.

  • • Materials – Cowhide, goatskin, lambskin, buffalo, PU synthetic leather; polyester quilted, mesh, or shearling linings
  • • Key features – Front zipper with storm flap, zippered cuffs, multiple pockets, removable thermal vests, armor pockets
  • • Sub-categories – Biker, flight, racer, bomber, and work leather jackets
  • • Typical users – Motorcyclists, warehouse workers, security personnel, fashion consumers, outdoor workers

Rain Wear

Waterproof garments designed to keep the wearer dry during precipitation. Available as jackets, trousers, overalls, and two-piece sets — critical for outdoor occupations and recreation.

  • • Materials – PVC, PU-coated polyester/nylon, Gore-Tex breathable membranes, soft shell with DWR finish
  • • Key features – Taped/welded seams, adjustable hoods and cuffs, underarm vents, reflective strips, storm flaps
  • • Sub-categories – Ponchos, long coats, rain suits, high-visibility rainwear, packable emergency rainsuits
  • • Typical users – Construction workers, fishermen, motorcyclists, cyclists, hikers, municipal and event staff

Sports Gloves

Hand coverings optimized for specific athletic activities, offering grip, palm protection, moisture management, and dexterity across a wide range of sports and fitness applications.

  • • Key features – Perforated palms, reinforced wear zones, touchscreen fingertips, neoprene wrist support, microfiber sweat wipe
  • • Sub-categories – Cycling, gym/weightlifting, golf, baseball, and motocross gloves
  • • Typical users – Gym goers, cyclists, golfers, baseball players, general fitness enthusiasts

Motorbike Gloves

Protective handwear specifically engineered for motorcycle riding, prioritizing abrasion resistance, impact protection, and handlebar grip. Available in seasonal variants for all riding conditions.

  • • Materials – Goat, cow, or kangaroo leather; Cordura/Kevlar textile; carbon fiber, TPU or D3O armor; fleece or Thinsulate lining
  • • Key features – Hard knuckle protectors, palm sliders, pre-curved fingers, touchscreen fingertips, double/triple stitching, waterproof membrane

Ski Gloves

Insulated, waterproof gloves for snow sports and cold-weather outdoor work, designed to maintain dexterity and warmth in sub-zero temperatures across all winter activity levels.

  • • Materials – Nylon/polyester or leather shell, Gore-Tex/Hipora/OutDry membrane, PrimaLoft or Thinsulate insulation, brushed tricot lining
  • • Key features – Long gauntlet cuffs, pre-curved fingers, reinforced palm and thumb, nose wipe panel, removable liner gloves, heated versions available
  • • Sub-categories – Traditional ski gloves, mittens, split finger gloves, backcountry/touring gloves, and kids' ski gloves
  • • Typical users – Alpine skiers, snowboarders, ski patrollers, snowmobile riders, winter construction and polar expedition staff

WORKWEAR MARKET INSIGHTS

Global Buyers & Workwear Applications

Understanding global demand patterns helps align sourcing, production, and long-term supply strategies.

Large-Volume Purchasers & Importers of Work Clothing

Major Retailers – Large chains such as Walmart, Target, and Amazon import workwear in bulk for both retail stores and online distribution.

Workwear Wholesalers – Suppliers serving construction, manufacturing, and industrial sectors manage high-volume imports to support continuous demand.

Workwear Producers – Manufacturers import fabrics and raw materials at scale to support mass production of industrial garments.

Government Bodies – Military, law enforcement, and public sector organizations procure workwear in bulk for uniforms and operational use.

Private Corporations – Companies import workwear for employee uniforms and safety compliance across various industries.

Types of Workwear Apparel

Coveralls & Overalls – Full-body protective garments widely used in construction, maintenance, and automotive work environments.

Work Trousers – Durable pants made from materials like denim or canvas, suitable for physically demanding tasks.

Work Shirts – Breathable and structured garments used across industrial, agricultural, and labor-intensive sectors.

High-Visibility Clothing – Reflective garments designed to improve worker safety in low-light or high-risk environments.
